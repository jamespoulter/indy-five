01
🏎️
A Proper Sports City
Indy 500, Pacers, Colts. Sport isn't a leisure activity here — it's civic identity, inherited and worn with pride.
Deeper Dive
Did You Know?
The World's Biggest Single-Day Sporting Event
The Indianapolis Motor Speedway holds 250,000+ fans — more than any other sporting venue on earth. The Indy 500 has run almost every year since 1911. The Pacers won three ABA titles before the merger. The Colts brought a Super Bowl in 2007. Sport here isn't a pastime — it's the civic glue.

02
🗺️
Surprisingly Easy to Navigate
Compact Mile Square grid. Walkable downtown. A 900k+ city that somehow doesn't sprawl into chaos.
Deeper Dive
Urban Design
One Square Mile That Actually Works
Indianapolis was designed on a modified L'Enfant grid — radiating streets from the Monument Circle centre point. You can walk from one end of downtown to the other in 20 minutes flat. No subway needed. The Cultural Trail (cycling & pedestrian) connects neighbourhoods beautifully. For a major American city, it's startlingly human-scale.

03
🍺
Food Scene Better Than Reputation
Great BBQ. Sun King craft beer (Indiana's largest). A growing independent restaurant culture that punches well above its weight.
Deeper Dive
Eat & Drink
Sun King, Smoked Meats & Serious Chefs
Sun King Brewing (founded 2009) became Indiana's largest craft brewery in record time — their Sunlight Cream Ale is iconic. The Fountain Square and Mass Ave neighbourhoods host award-winning independent restaurants. Indy's BBQ scene rivals cities with twice its reputation. Don't sleep on the tenderloin sandwich — it's a state institution.
Back
04
🏢
Serious Convention & Event Hub
More hotel rooms per capita than almost any US city. NCAA Final Four. Big Ten events. The infrastructure to host anything.
Deeper Dive
Infrastructure
Built to Host. And It Shows.
Indianapolis has methodically built some of the best convention infrastructure in North America. The Indiana Convention Center connects directly to multiple hotels via skywalks. The city has hosted Super Bowls, the NCAA Final Four multiple times, and major political conventions. With ~4,800 hotel rooms connected to the convention centre, it's a logistics dream for event planners.

05
🌆
Genuinely Mid-American
Honest, unpretentious, working-class in its bones. Real inequality and urban challenges. A city figuring out what it becomes next.
Deeper Dive
The Real Indy
Unvarnished, Unfinished, Authentic
Indianapolis doesn't perform authenticity — it just is. There are deep racial and economic divides, particularly on the east and northwest sides. Downtown investment has been uneven. But there's a genuine civic conversation happening: how do you build a city that works for everyone? That honesty is part of what makes Indy worth paying attention to. It's a test case for the American Midwest.

Bonus Facts

Did You Know?

Swipe or click through for surprising Indy facts.

Fact 01 / 07
Indianapolis hosts more hotel rooms connected to its convention centre than any other city in North America — perfect for marathon-length conferences.
Fact 02 / 07
The Indy 500 has been run every year since 1911 — with the sole exception of 1917–1918 and 1942–1945 due to the World Wars.
Fact 03 / 07
Sun King Brewing, founded in 2009, became Indiana's largest craft brewery within just three years of opening.
Fact 04 / 07
The Indianapolis Motor Speedway oval is so large that the Vatican, Yankee Stadium, Churchill Downs, the Rose Bowl, and the Roman Colosseum could all fit inside it — simultaneously.
Fact 05 / 07
The Indiana War Memorial Plaza is the second largest memorial plaza in the United States, after Washington D.C.'s National Mall.
Fact 06 / 07
Indianapolis was the first US city to build a public water filtration plant, back in 1870 — making clean drinking water a civic priority before almost anywhere else.
Fact 07 / 07
The "Naptown" nickname (now a point of local pride) used to be a slur for the city's slow pace. Now it's reclaimed as a badge of authentic Midwestern identity.
