Indianapolis The Crossroads of America

Five Things to Know

KnowThe City.

Indianapolis doesn't need to shout. It just shows up. Here's what you actually need to know about Indiana's capital.

01

It's a Proper Sports City — Not Just Hype

The Indianapolis Motor Speedway is one of the most iconic venues in world sport. The Indy 500 is the largest single-day sporting event on the planet. The Pacers and Colts both have passionate fanbases. Sport isn't an add-on here — it's woven into the city's identity.

02

It's Surprisingly Easy to Navigate

Compact downtown, walkable Mile Square grid, most things you'd want within a short radius. For a city of 900k+ people it punches well above its weight for ease of getting around. No sprawling chaos — just a clean, functional city layout.

03

The Food Scene Is Better Than the Reputation Suggests

Most people write it off. They're wrong. Particularly good for BBQ, craft beer (Sun King is excellent), and there's a growing independent restaurant culture that doesn't get enough credit. Give it a chance before you write it off.

04

It's a Serious Convention and Event Hub

More hotel rooms per capita than almost any US city. Hosts the NCAA Final Four, Big Ten events, and major conferences regularly. The infrastructure is built for it — which is why serious events keep choosing Indianapolis over flashier alternatives.

05

It's Genuinely Mid-American — in the Best and Hardest Senses

Honest, unpretentious, and working-class in its bones — which is refreshing. But it also carries real inequality and urban challenges that don't get enough national attention. It's a city trying to figure out what it becomes next. An interesting place to watch.
